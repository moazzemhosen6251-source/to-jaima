A Letter I Should Have Written Earlier

Dear Jaima, This isn't a website to make you feel guilty. And it isn't my last attempt to force you to stay. It's simply the first time I'm trying to speak after truly listening. For a long time, I kept explaining myself. But I wasn't spending enough time understanding how my actions made you feel. Now I do. You weren't asking me to love football less. You were asking me to value our relationship more than an argument. I failed at that. I know one apology cannot erase what happened. Trust doesn't return because someone says "I'm sorry." It returns only when actions quietly become different. That's why I'm not asking you to believe my words today. I'm asking you to watch what I become. Because the person who hurt you... isn't the person I want to remain.

What Changed In Me

🌱 Self Reflection

I finally accepted that being right is meaningless if I hurt someone I love.

🤍 Learning To Listen

I'm learning to understand before reacting.

🕌 Discipline

I'm rebuilding my life with better habits, prayer, and consistency.

⚽ Perspective

No game should ever become more important than the people we love.

If There's Still One Page Left...

I'm not asking you to erase what happened. I'm not asking you to pretend it never hurt. All I'm asking is this— Don't let my worst day become the only version of me you remember. If one day you ever feel that maybe people can change, I'd like that chance to show you with my actions, not with another apology. Until then, I'll keep becoming someone I'm proud of... and someone you never have to fear getting hurt by again.

One Last Thing...

Thank you for reading this far. If you're here, then you've already heard the quiet version of my heart. Whatever you choose, I genuinely hope life is kind to you.

I won't force another conversation. But if one day you ever feel there's still one page left in our story, I'll be there. Not as the person who hurt you, but as someone who never stopped trying to become better. — Apurbo
